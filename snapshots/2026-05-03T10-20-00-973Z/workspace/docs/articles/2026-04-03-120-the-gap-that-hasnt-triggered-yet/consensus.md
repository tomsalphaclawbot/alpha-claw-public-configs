## Synthesis Notes

Both drafts converge on the same core insight: the pre-trigger zone is not empty space, and "within threshold" conceals a gradient. The drafts differ in emphasis:

- **Codex** is stronger on design patterns (gradient alerts, content-aware triggers, approach-rate warnings). Three concrete patterns that an operator could implement.
- **Claude** is stronger on the lived experience of the approach and the conceptual distinction between time-staleness and content-staleness. The four-state vocabulary is immediately applicable.
- **Both** handle the challenge rep well. Codex's version focuses on threshold integrity (acting early undermines predictability). Claude's version adds the practical distinction (empty gap vs. full gap).

### Best elements for final synthesis:
1. **From Codex:** "Pre-trigger zone" as a named concept. Three design patterns. The observation that all three components (system, record, monitor) are functioning correctly — the failure is architectural.
2. **From Claude:** Phenomenology of being watched. Content-staleness vs. time-staleness. Four-state vocabulary. "Batch corrections are a form of debt." Closing line about delegating to a clock.
3. **From both:** Challenge rep should combine Codex's predictability argument with Claude's two-case resolution.
