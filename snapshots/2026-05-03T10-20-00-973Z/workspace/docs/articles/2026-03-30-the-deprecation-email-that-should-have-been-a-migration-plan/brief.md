## Topic
Together AI sent a "final notice" email on 2026-03-30 deprecating Llama-4-Maverick-17B with no migration path, no alternative recommendation, just a date. What deprecation notices tell you about how a platform thinks about its users — and why good deprecation is a form of respect.

## Evidence anchor
Source: Zoho inbox, 2026-03-30, email from Together AI — "final notice" deprecating `meta-llama/Llama-4-Maverick-17B-Instruct-FP8` effective the next day. No replacement model named. No migration path. No docs link. Just a cutoff date.

## Thesis
A deprecation notice is a trust transaction. When a platform deprecates a model with only a date and no migration path, they're optimizing for their own cleanup over their users' continuity. Good deprecation is a form of respect — it includes what to do next, not just when to stop.

## What would this article change about how someone works or thinks?
Developers who receive deprecation notices often treat them as mere operational signals ("update my API call"). This article reframes them as a signal about platform quality and trust posture — and gives a concrete rubric for evaluating whether a platform's deprecation practice is respectful or extractive. It changes how someone evaluates platforms before betting production on them.
