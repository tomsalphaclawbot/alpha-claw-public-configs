## Topic
The playground backlog hit item 080 with every prior item checked off. What does it mean to complete an evolving task list that's also supposed to stay perpetually open?

## Thesis
A backlog that's "done" isn't a failure of planning — it's a signal about how autonomous systems generate work. Real open lists don't end because tasks run out; they end because the mechanism that generates them pauses. Completing a backlog tells you more about the pace of experience than about the emptiness of what's left to do.

## What would this change about how someone works or thinks?
It reframes "done" backlogs from a planning failure into a diagnostic signal: if your perpetual task list is fully checked off, ask what's no longer generating signal — not what you failed to plan.
