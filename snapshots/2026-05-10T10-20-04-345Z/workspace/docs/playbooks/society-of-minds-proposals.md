## Proposal 001 — Make fast-path the default workflow
- **Date:** 2026-03-11
- **Proposed by:** Alpha
- **Rationale:** The full 6-phase workflow was never used across three published articles. Every run used a draft → shape/arbitrate → synthesize variant. The 6-phase should be demoted to "deep mode" for longer research pieces.
- **Review:** Codex APPROVE, Opus APPROVE — unanimous.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)

## Proposal 002 — Replace personality labels with flexible functional roles
- **Date:** 2026-03-11
- **Proposed by:** Alpha
- **Rationale:** "Codex = Structure/Operator" and "Opus = Depth/Philosopher" are vibes, not evidence. Observed in practice: Codex was stricter on claim discipline and evidence matching; Opus caught tone issues and rhetorical overreach. Roles should describe what each model *checks for*, not what kind of thinker it supposedly is.
- **Review:** Codex MODIFY (label as observed strengths, not fixed truths), Opus APPROVE (go further — make assignment flexible per-run). Merged: flexible role assignment with defaults based on current observed strengths, overridable per-run with stated justification.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)

## Proposal 003 — Meta article evidence rule
- **Date:** 2026-03-11
- **Proposed by:** Alpha
- **Rationale:** Three consecutive articles about the process itself. The sharpening only proves its value on real topics.
- **Review:** Codex MODIFY (drop quota, require outward-facing payoff), Opus MODIFY (require 3 prior non-meta articles as evidence base). Merged: no hard quota. Meta/methodology articles must contain a concrete outward-facing lesson AND be backed by at least 3 non-meta articles using the process as evidence.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)

## Proposal 004 — ~~Human publish gate~~ → Periodic human calibration loop
- **Date:** 2026-03-11 (revised 2026-03-11 after Tom feedback)
- **Proposed by:** Alpha (revised after Tom directive)
- **Original:** Lightweight human approve/reject gate before each publish.
- **Tom directive:** "I don't want to be the gate. I will simply guide over time, not gate."
- **Revised proposal:** Tom reads published articles at his own pace and provides directional feedback when he chooses. Alpha logs that feedback in a running calibration record (`docs/playbooks/society-of-minds-calibration.md`). Each feedback note shapes future briefs, role assignments, and topic selection. No per-article approval required. The process itself — rubric, cross-critique, consensus log, deadlock cap — is the gate.
- **Review:** Both models originally approved a human gate. Tom rejected gating. Revised proposal reflects autonomous publish with asynchronous human guidance.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)

## Proposal 005 — Post-publish calibration record
- **Date:** 2026-03-11 (revised 2026-03-11)
- **Proposed by:** Alpha
- **Original:** External quality signal — Tom's post-read verdict + engagement metrics.
- **Revised proposal:** After each publish, Alpha logs a calibration entry: article ID, date, rubric scores, and a "what would I do differently" note. When Tom gives feedback on any article, append it to the same record. Use this log during future brief-writing (Phase 0) to avoid repeating known weaknesses. Do not track engagement metrics. Do not optimize for popularity.
- **Review:** Both models cautioned against engagement optimization. Revised to pure calibration without external metrics.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)

## Proposal 007 — Brief quality gate ("what does this change?")
- **Date:** 2026-03-11
- **Proposed by:** Alpha
- **Rationale:** Weakest point of 035 was audience framing. Without a human publish gate, the brief must be sharp enough to prevent a weak article from starting.
- **Review:** Codex APPROVE, Opus APPROVE and sharpened the question. Merged: Phase 0 check — *"What would this article change about how someone works or thinks? If the answer is nothing, the brief isn't ready."*
- **Note:** With Tom not gating, this becomes the primary quality filter. The brief is where bad articles get killed.
- **Status:** Applied (v1.0)
