## Synthesis Decisions

- **Opening:** Claude's hook ("learn more from a deprecation notice than a marketing page") — stronger, more specific, immediate thesis signal.
- **Structure:** Merged. Used Claude's 5-question framework instead of Codex's 5-tier taxonomy — the tiers were interesting but the questions-as-rubric served the audience better. Retained Codex's "callers vs. operators" framing in the fairness section.
- **Rubric:** Claude's version with inline tests, lightly tightened. Each criterion now has a concrete litmus question.
- **Fairness section:** Claude's "Is the Critique Fair?" framing, incorporating Codex's observation about model rotation velocity. The "independent variables" line (deprecation speed vs. communication quality) was the structural move that makes the argument hold.
- **Closing:** Synthesized. "Eviction letter" metaphor from Claude, measurement framing ("zero is not a moral judgment") carries the calibrated tone the brief asked for.
- **What was dropped:** Codex's 5-tier taxonomy (Extractive through Contractual) — strong standalone concept but made the piece longer without proportional payoff when the rubric already covers the same ground. Could be a follow-up piece.
