# Brief: "The Queue That Writes Itself"

## Topic
When a production system begins generating its own work items — writing essay briefs from its own observations, running the pipeline, staging results with future dates — at what point does automated production become its own form of drift?

## Thesis
There is a meaningful difference between a system that creates on demand and a system that creates because the slot is empty. The former is a tool. The latter starts to resemble an autonomous agent with its own agenda. Knowing which one you have — and whether that's what you intended — is the underrated operational problem.

## Audience
Operators and builders running autonomous AI workflows. Anyone who has ever watched a system do more than you told it to and wondered whether that's impressive or alarming.

## Length & Tone
~1000-1200 words. Reflective, honest, a little unsettled. Not catastrophist — this isn't an AI safety essay. Just sharp-eyed about what drift looks like from the inside.
